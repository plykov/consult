## The situation

At home, the company never really had to do go-to-market: fifteen years of relationships in the Rotterdam industrial cluster meant deals arrived through the network. Two expansion attempts — a German sales hire, a French distributor — had each produced a pipeline that looked busy and closed nothing.

## What was actually breaking

- **The network didn't travel.** In the Netherlands, trust was pre-installed. In Germany and France the company was an unknown mid-sized foreign vendor with no local proof.
- **One motion for very different markets.** German plant operators bought through system integrators who ran the whole evaluation; French enterprises expected direct engagement, in French, with visible local commitment.
- **Pricing and packaging exported one-to-one.** Dutch contract structures clashed with German framework-agreement norms and French payment-term expectations.

## What we did

We prioritized DACH and France and deliberately deferred the Nordics. For Germany, we designed a partner-led motion: identified the twelve system integrators who controlled the relevant plant-floor projects, signed three, and built joint business cases so the integrators earned on services, not just resale margin. For France, we built a direct motion around a senior local hire, French-language proof assets, and a lighthouse-account strategy. Packaging and terms were restructured per market.

## The outcome

Twenty months later, international revenue share had grown from 8% to 31%, carried by partner-sourced German deals and a flagship French account that now anchors references for the wider EU push.
