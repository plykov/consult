## The situation

The vendor had strong momentum at home and a repeatable outbound machine: SDR sequences, security-leader dinners, POC-driven closes. In France, the same machine generated polite first meetings that never converted. The internal read was "the French market is slow." It wasn't.

## What was actually breaking

- **Deals were shaped before the vendor arrived.** French enterprise security purchases were heavily influenced by incumbent MSSPs and a small circle of consulting advisors. Shortlists were effectively drafted inside those relationships.
- **Trust signals were misaligned.** No French customers, no French-language materials, no local entity — each one a quiet disqualifier in risk-averse security procurement.
- **Outbound read as noise.** Cold sequences to CISOs who buy through trusted intermediaries damaged the brand more than they built pipeline.

## What we did

We mapped partner influence across the target segment: which MSSPs held the managed-security contracts, which advisors ran vendor evaluations. We selected two anchor MSSP partnerships, designed a commercial model where the partner owned the service wrapper, and co-built French-language proof assets including a joint reference architecture. Outbound was stopped entirely; the motion became partner-sourced introductions backed by technical co-selling.

## The outcome

The first six enterprise deals all arrived through the two anchor partners, at a customer acquisition cost 45% below the direct attempts — and the vendor is now shortlisted in national tenders it previously never heard about.
