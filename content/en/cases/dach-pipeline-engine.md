## The situation

The company had grown to ~$40M ARR in North America on a classic developer-led motion: bottom-up adoption, a single engineering champion, a fast commercial close. In Germany, the same motion produced enthusiastic users — and zero closed enterprise deals in 14 months.

## What was actually breaking

The diagnostic showed the product was loved and the pricing was acceptable. The failure was structural:

- **The decision unit was invisible to them.** German enterprise deals involved six to ten stakeholders — engineering, security, legal, purchasing, and in two accounts a works council. The playbook engaged exactly one of them.
- **Procurement was treated as paperwork.** Data-residency clauses and framework-agreement requirements surfaced at contract stage and reset deals by months.
- **Reference logic ran backwards.** US logos impressed nobody; buyers asked "who runs this in production in Germany?" and the answer was no one.

## What we did

Over a twelve-week engagement we mapped the decision units in the six live opportunities, built stakeholder-specific proof for security and legal, re-sequenced the sales process so procurement requirements were qualified in discovery instead of discovered at signature, and turned the two most advanced users into a structured German reference program.

## The outcome

The first two six-figure deals closed within seven months. More importantly, the team could now forecast DACH deals with the same confidence as US ones, because the process finally matched how their German customers actually buy.
