Every quarter we meet a team with the same story: a European deal, verbally won, that has been "in procurement" for five months. The conclusion they draw is that European procurement is slow. The truth is harder: the deal was lost in discovery, and procurement is just where the body was found.

**European procurement is not late-stage paperwork.** Framework agreements, data-residency requirements, payment-term norms, tender thresholds, supplier-qualification processes — these differ by country and are *knowable in advance*. A German framework agreement, a French *appel d'offres*, a Dutch DORA-driven vendor assessment: none of these should ever surprise you in month six.

**Three practical moves:**

1. **Qualify procurement in the first three calls.** Ask: "When you last bought software in this category, what did the contracting process look like, and who ran it?" The answer tells you the real timeline and the real requirements — and buyers respect the question.
2. **Pre-build the artifacts.** A GDPR-ready data-processing agreement, an EU hosting answer, a security questionnaire response pack, references from the same country. Having these ready compresses months into weeks.
3. **Make procurement a champion.** Procurement teams are measured on risk and process quality. Vendors who make their job easy — clean documentation, straight answers, no pressure tactics — get pulled forward into the next deal. In several of our cases, the second sale was sourced by procurement.

Teams that treat procurement as a stakeholder from day one don't just close faster. They build a reputation inside the buying organization that outlasts the individual deal.
